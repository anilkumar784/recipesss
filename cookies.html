What Are Cookies?

Cookies are small text files stored on your device (computer, mobile phone, or tablet) when you visit a website. They help improve your browsing experience by remembering your preferences and actions over time. Cookies serve various functions, from essential site operation to analytics and advertising.

Types of cookies commonly used include:

  • Session Cookies: Temporary cookies that expire when you close your browser, enabling basic site functions.
  • Persistent Cookies: Remain on your device for a set period or until manually deleted, remembering your preferences on return visits.
  • First-Party Cookies: Set directly by the website you are visiting.
  • Third-Party Cookies: Set by external services or partners providing additional functionality, such as analytics or advertising.

Managing and Disabling Cookies

You have the right to control the use of cookies on your device. Most browsers automatically accept cookies, but you can usually modify your settings to decline or delete cookies if you wish.

Common browser options include:

  • Disabling Cookies: Prevent websites from storing cookies on your device.
  • Deleting Cookies: Remove stored cookies manually or set browsers to clear them on exit.
  • Managing Preferences: Limit cookies to specific sites or choose which categories of cookies to allow.

Please note that disabling certain cookies may affect your user experience, impair site functionality, or prevent access to some services.
